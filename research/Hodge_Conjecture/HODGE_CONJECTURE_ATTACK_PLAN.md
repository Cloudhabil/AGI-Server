## HODGE CONJECTURE: THE PROBLEM

### Formal Statement

**Definition**: Let X be a complex projective variety of dimension n. The Hodge conjecture states:

Every Hodge class on X is a rational linear combination of cohomology classes of algebraic cycles.

**Mathematically**:
```
Hodge^{p,p}(X) ⊗ ℚ = Im[H^{2p}(X, ℤ) → H^{2p}(X, ℚ)]
```

Where:
- Hodge^{p,p}(X) = harmonic differential forms of type (p,p)
- These are topologically defined (not algebraically!)
- The conjecture: ALL such forms come from algebraic cycles

### Why It Matters

The Hodge conjecture is about whether **topology determines algebra**:
- Can we algebraically describe all topological structure?
- Or does topology contain "extra" information?
- The answer determines the nature of algebraic varieties
