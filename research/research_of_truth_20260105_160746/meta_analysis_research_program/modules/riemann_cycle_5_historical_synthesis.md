## 3. THE CORE OBSTACLE: UNIFIED CHARACTERIZATION

### What Every Approach Must Face

**The fundamental problem all approaches must solve:**

Given:
- Zeta function ζ(s) is well-defined throughout complex plane
- Zeros exist off critical line mathematically (as far as we can prove)
- Functional equation is satisfied

Prove:
- No zeros actually occur off critical line
- Every single one of infinitely many zeros lies on Re(s)=1/2

**Why this is hard:**

1. **Local vs. Global Mismatch**
   - All tools prove local properties (few zeros, bounded regions)
   - Need to prove global constraint (infinite set, all zeros)
   - Logical gap between local and global

2. **Negative vs. Positive Proof**
   - Easy to prove: "Some zeros have Re(s)=1/2"
   - Hard to prove: "All zeros have Re(s)=1/2"
   - Hard to prove: "None have Re(s)≠1/2"
   - Requires ruling out infinite possibility space

3. **Infinity Problem**
   - Cannot verify infinitely many cases
   - Induction from finite to infinite is non-trivial
   - Every finite verification leaves infinite remainder

4. **Uniqueness Problem**
   - Why should zeros cluster exactly on one line?
   - Why not nearby lines (Re(s)=1/2±ε)?
   - Why not spread across strip?
   - No known principle forces this uniqueness
