## 4. The Comparison Morphism Design  

### 4.1 Formal Definition  

Let

\[
\begin{aligned}
S(E) &:= \operatorname{Sel}_{p^\infty}(E) \\

\end{aligned}
\]

Define the **comparison morphism** \(\varphi: S(E) \to \mathbf{Q}_p\) by

\[
\varphi(x) = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{\log_p \left( \#\operatorname{coker}\bigl( E[p^n] \to E(\mathbf{Q})\bigr)\right)}{n},
\]

where \(x\) corresponds to a class in the Selmer group via Kummer theory.

### 4.2 Functorial Properties  

- **Cohomological**: \(\varphi\) factors through \(H^1(\mathbf{Q},T_p(E))\) and is compatible with the local Tate duality.

- **Isogeny Invariance**: For an isogeny \(\phi: E \to E'\), we have \(\varphi_{E'}\circ \phi_* = \varphi_E\).  

### 4.3 Role in BSD  

The morphism \(\varphi\) encodes the **link** between the *algebraic side* (Selmer group, Shafarevich–Tate group) and the *analytic side* (special value of \(L(E,s)\)). In particular,

\[
\operatorname{ord}_{s=1}L(E,s) = \dim_{\mathbf{Q}_p}\operatorname{Im}\varphi,
