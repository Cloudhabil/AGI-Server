## The Answer (Physics Consensus)

### Part 1: Minimum Dimensionless Constants

**The Fundamental Answer**: **3 to 4 independent dimensionless constants**

These are typically:

1. **Fine Structure Constant (α)** ≈ 1/137.036
   - Characterizes electromagnetic coupling strength
   - α = e²/(4πε₀ℏc) in SI units
   - Dimensionless by nature

2. **Weak Interaction Coupling** (αw or sin²θw) ≈ 0.23
   - Characterizes weak nuclear force coupling
   - Related to W boson mass and Higgs coupling

3. **Strong Interaction Coupling** (αs) ≈ 0.118
   - Characterizes strong nuclear force (gluon) coupling
   - Running coupling that changes with energy scale

4. **Gravitational Coupling** (αG or dimensionless variant)
   - If included, requires Newton's constant G
   - Often omitted from "Standard Model" dimensionless constants
   - Would be ~10⁻⁸ scale in natural units

### Why These Three (or Four)?

These cannot be derived from first principles in the Standard Model—they are **empirical inputs**. However:
- All OTHER dimensionless ratios (mass ratios, mixing angles, etc.) CAN be expressed as functions of these
- Example: electron mass / muon mass ratio is derivable from coupling constants and Higgs sector
- Example: CKM matrix elements (flavor mixing) related to fundamental couplings

## Part 2: The Unit System Question

### What Happens in Planck Units?

When you set: **c = 1, ℏ = 1, G = 1** (Planck units)

**Result**:
- All constants become dimensionless ratios
- Dimensional "constants" (c, ℏ, G) are just unit conversion factors
- The three coupling constants (α, αw, αs) remain

### Conclusion: Are Dimensional Constants Necessary?

**Answer: NO** - They are **not fundamental**

**Reasoning**:
1. **Dimensional constants are human artifacts** arising from arbitrary unit choices
   - Set c = 1 → removes need for speed of light
   - Set ℏ = 1 → removes need for Planck's constant
   - Set G = 1 → removes need for gravitational constant

2. **Nature operates with only dimensionless ratios**
   - The universe doesn't "know" about meters, seconds, kilograms
   - Only ratios between quantities matter (mass ratios, coupling strengths)
   - A physicist could work entirely in natural units

3. **Physics is fundamentally scale-invariant up to quantum effects**
   - Electromagnetic coupling runs with energy (α varies from ~1/137 to ~1/127)
   - Strong coupling runs dramatically (αs from ~0.1 to ~0.12)
   - But these ratios are what matter, not absolute "constants"
